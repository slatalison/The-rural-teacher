He knew that this would be his last class.
His liver hurt badly: he could feel that his organs were gradually shutting down.
He and his students lived at school since the nearest home of students was tens miles of mountain road away.
Those tanned, short, skinny 7-year-olds were the future of the mountain.
He had tried his best to lit the candle of knowledge in this hopeless mountain,
and tonight he had to end, if possible, to finish his attempt.
